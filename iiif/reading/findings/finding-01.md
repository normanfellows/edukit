# Finding 01 — The Available Position

## Experiment 02 — Reading Edukit

### Finding

Edukit appears to develop not primarily through the addition of resolved architectural content, but through the construction of an ordering field in which relationships, substitutions and future contributions can occur.

The three documentary traces assembled in the IIIF manifest suggest a progression.

In `item_00002`, Edukit is represented as a systems image. Geographically dispersed points, links and concentrations are assembled into a single visual field. The image is incomplete, but its principal concern appears already to be relational. What matters is not an individual architectural object but the organisation of connections across a larger system.

In `item_00151`, the systems image is repeated within a field of earlier documentary material concerned with links, two-way communication, specialist connection and group organisation. Its reuse invites the earlier material to be read not simply as historical precedent, but as part of the developing logic of Edukit. The systems image appears to rescale a communications structure from facility to planetary system.

In `item_00148`, the logic of organisation becomes explicit as a field of twenty-four positions arranged in six rows of four. Some positions are occupied; others remain blank.

NF recalls that the blanks were both unfinished spaces and available spaces: positions awaiting conceptually appropriate material within a predetermined chromatic and spatial arrangement. The colour arrangement, inherited from Medikit Fantasia, was treated as sacrosanct. Images could be chromatically altered in order to negotiate their placement within the field.

A comparison is provided by NF's interview with Cedric Price at 38 Alfred Place on 23 July 1996. Price proposed an ordering on one sheet in which “big blanks” might remain because “something's gonna happen there, but I don't know”. Another participant might then propose: “that blank I'd put this”. NF characterised the procedure as “a game”; Price agreed.

## Finding 01

**The blank may be understood as an active component of Edukit's system of ordering. It marks not simply the absence of a proposition, but the availability of a position for a proposition not yet determined.**

Edukit's systems logic may therefore lie partly in its capacity to make somewhere for things to go.

The system does not need to know in advance what every position will contain. Instead, it establishes a field sufficiently ordered to receive, compare and reposition future material.

On this reading, incompleteness is not necessarily a failure of resolution. It may be a condition of exchange.

### Caution

This finding does not claim that NF consciously formulated a theory of open systems or participatory incompleteness when preparing `item_00148`.

The documentary trace and NF's recollection support a narrower proposition: the positions were awaiting completion, but the structure of the field allowed that completion to remain undetermined.

The 1996 exchange with Cedric Price is therefore used as a comparison rather than as a retrospective claim of causation.
