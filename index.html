NF EDUKIT SYSTEMS

A systems repository for distributed educational servicing, developed from Edukit, Thinkbelt, Atom, and related work on anticipatory infrastructure, commons governance, and kit-of-parts educational environments.

The page is intentionally minimal: an index, a navigation surface, and a stable frame for future entries, diagrams, and catalogue objects.

Territories

Geographies of educational servicing and regional transformation.

Regional contexts

Potteries, Dukeries, Atomia, Cambridge–Milton Keynes, Oxford, CAMKOX, and the wider question of global imbalance in educational provision.

Territorial logic

The repository treats territory as a service condition: not merely location, but a field for distribution, access, and infrastructural change.

Timelines

Life cycles, anticipation, inheritance, and recursive development.

1964 → 1968

Potteries Thinkbelt and Atom US establish early models for educational servicing beyond the conventional university.

1996 → present

Edukit develops as a continuing work in progress, with the repository acting as a living index.

2014 → present

Dukeries Thinkbelt and Atom UK extend the logic into later regional and territorial studies.

Use cycle

Obsolescence, adaptation, removal, and replacement are treated as design conditions rather than failures.
